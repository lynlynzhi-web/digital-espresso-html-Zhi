History

Arabica
A high-quality coffee species known for its smooth, mild flavor and aromatic profile. Grown mainly in high-altitude regions, it has lower bitterness and acidity than other coffee types, making it a favorite for specialty coffee blends worldwide.
Robusta
a coffee species recognized for its strong, bold flavor and higher caffeine content compared to Arabica. It has a more bitter, earthy taste and is often used in espresso blends and instant coffees. Robusta plants are hardier, resistant to pests, and thrive at lower altitudes and warmer climates.
Fair Trade
a global certification system that ensures farmers and workers in developing countries receive fair wages and work under safe, ethical conditions. It promotes sustainable farming practices, community development, and environmental responsibility, allowing consumers to support social and economic justice through their purchases.
